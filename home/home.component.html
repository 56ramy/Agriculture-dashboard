AGRICULTURE

Agriculture is a large sector in India, accounting for approximately 16% of the country's GDP and employing more than 50% of the workforce. The agriculture sector in India is diversified, with crops such as rice, wheat, maize, pulses, cotton, sugarcane, and fruits and vegetables.It plays a vital role in the country's food security and rural development. Indian government has launched various initiatives to support agriculture and farmers, such as the Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (crop insurance scheme), Kisan Credit Card scheme, and e-NAM (National Agriculture Market) platform for online trading of agricultural produce. The government also provides subsidies for inputs like seeds, fertilizers, and irrigation.

Cashewnut

The cashew tree (Anacardium occidentale) is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America in the genus Anacardium that produces the cashew seed and the cashew apple accessory fruit. The tree can grow as tall as 14 metres (46 feet), but the dwarf cultivars, growing up to 6 m (20 ft), prove more profitable, with earlier maturity and greater yields. The cashew seed is commonly considered a snack nut (cashew nut) eaten on its own, used in recipes, or processed into cashew cheese or cashew butter.Like the tree, the nut is often simply called a cashew. Cashew allergies are triggered by the proteins found in tree nuts, and cooking often does not remove or change these proteins
